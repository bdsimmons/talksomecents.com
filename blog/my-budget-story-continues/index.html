My Budget Story Continues: You Will Always Fail at Money Without a Plan

April 30, 2017 Nathan Lippincott

3 minute read

  • Twitter

  • Google+

  • Facebook

  • Reddit

  • LinkedIn

  • StumbleUpon

  • Email

It isn’t how much money you make that matters but rather the plan that you make with that money. My family and I have used many different products to budget. We have done a budget on paper. We have used phone applications. We have used websites. The only criterion for choosing the best tool for your budget is to ensure that it works for you and your family and that you will use it!

Because the number one goal in budgeting is to make sure every dollar has a job to do, an effective tool is one that allows you to see everything in action in order to achieve the equation, “Income – Expense =$0.” Otherwise, you are doing the budget incorrectly. If using a pencil and paper makes this achievable for you, or if you work best with a fancier Excel spreadsheet, or even if you want to find a software program or phone app, it really does not matter. Just ensure that you are working on your budget in some manner at least once a month.

In future blogs, I will review several of the most popular budgeting tools that are available and evaluate their effectiveness and ease of use. But in this segment of my own personal budget story, I will just state that my favorite out of all of these options is the program, You Need a Budget (YNAB). I call it my glorified checking account. It is very easy to use. The thing I like about it is that it keeps you very organized and you cannot overspend if you are implementing the strategy correctly. YNAB consists of various categories, both income and expenditure related, and is accessible on both a computer and individual smart phones. Once you enter the values into each budget category, you can see at a glance exactly where your money is and where it needs to go. By updating the budget regularly (even immediately after a purchase, when using the smart phone app), it is possible to know the exact amount that remains in each category. For example, instead of thinking that you have $5,000 in your checking account and spending without foresight, by using YNAB, you can look at your categories on the computer or phone and realize that there is only $200 in the Eating Out category, or $450 in the Groceries category. This allows you to spend within your budget, limits overspending, and keeps you in control.

Within six to nine months of following the YNAB budget, I was a month ahead on my bills. This was amazing, considering that during the first eight years of my marriage, I had been living paycheck to paycheck. YNAB helped me to form significant money habits that I continue to this day. Each month, I spend 10 minutes with my budget, accessing the YNAB app and creating categories and expenses for next four weeks. In the beginning, I failed miserable at this. I overspent and I forgot categories. But the one thing that kept me going was the knowledge that if I was overspending WITH a budget, I would have been spending twice that amount WITHOUT one.
